Scientific Challenge:

The current state of the art in drug discovery is based around molecular modelling but struggles to harness all of the data available. Structural biology and medicinal chemistry together produce a very large amount of data, but current approaches discard much of these data; for example, the molecular design is generally focused on individual hypotheses that are often informed by a subset of available structural information. In almost all cases, we know that the data are not fully interrogated or exploited to improve the processes underpinning the development of drug candidates. Likewise, mass spectrometry, electron microscopy and imaging with sound and light generate high data volumes where pattern recognition and data analysis could reap more useful information.
The Institute is being closely involved, alongside campus partners Diamond Light Source and STFC, in projects such as XChem, the high-throughput crystallography facility at Diamond. Coupled with this is a major collaborative effort in re-imagining medicinal chemistry, focussing on new chemistries, on a smaller scale, with more automated approaches.
The Head of Machine Learning will be expected to work closely with RFI leadership to identify community needs, new opportunities and streams of funding as novel technologies are developed.

Appointment:
The post holder can work at Harwell or be based in one of the partner Universities with an expectation of regular periods at Harwell. The appointment is possible by a range of routes, either by direct employment, secondment from industry or by a fellowship arrangement with a partner Institute. A range of flexible working options is available.

Role Requirements:

PhD (either obtained or thesis submitted) in machine learning / artificial intelligence, or a mathematical discipline with strong evidence of work in ML/AI. (A PhD in chemistry or structural biology IS NOT required, only an interest in the application of machine learning to the interrogation of large datasets to address problems in structural biology and molecular design)
Strong interpersonal skills
Demonstrable capability to build effective networks
Capacity to quickly understand and be able to add value to complex scientific problems
